Artworks

What Makes Us Human? (2024)

This 7 minute video explores the blur between the real and the virtual worlds, using 3D models of people I know combined with a 360 degree environment. Accompanied by ambient music made on Logic Pro, it reflects on human identity, technological advancement, and the uncertainty of the future, highlighting the uncanny and the unknown in a digital context.

Sims2 (2023)

This video explores the blur between the real and the virtual by combining The Sims visuals with a custom soundtrack created in Logic Pro. The triptych format and layered music create an immersive, slightly distorted atmosphere, highlighting the interplay between digital environments and narrative experience while reflecting on how virtual worlds can reshape perception and storytelling.

Sea Levels Rising (2023)

This video explores the impact of rising sea levels on London, focusing on Sheerness as a key indicator of climate change and the Thames Barrier closures. Using generative visuals and custom coded sounds created in Pure Data, I layered data driven audio with underwater inspired effects to highlight the increasing risk and urgency of climate change. The video gradually evolves in tone and saturation, reflecting the growing danger and encouraging awareness of our planet’s fragile future.

Untitled 1 (2023)

This video showcases an interactive project I created using JavaScript. The piece responds to user input moving the mouse changes how many generated images from a YouTube dataset are displayed on screen.

Untitled 2 (2023)

This video demonstrates an interactive artwork I created using P5.js, Arduino, and a distance sensor. The projection of colored rectangles responds to a viewer’s proximity, exploring the relationship between our physical presence and our digital self. While this video shows the project on a laptop, the final installation is designed for a gallery setting, where the sensor subtly alters the projected image, creating a playful and engaging interaction.
